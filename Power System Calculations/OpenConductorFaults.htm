Open Conductor Faults

Open conductor faults are series faults in a power system where one, two or even all three conductors of a power system element are disconnected, creating new unbalanced or balanced system state. It is a series fault unlike short circuits, which are shunt faults. The next figure illustrates all three cases:

  1. Regular or Full - LU decomposition of Jacobian matrix is performed at each iteration.
  2. Modified - LU decomposition of Jacobian matrix is performed only at initial iteration.
  3. Combined - LU decomposition of Jacobian matrix is performed only at the initial iteration and at subsequent iterations in which convergence rate drops below a specified threshold. This method is a combination of the previous two and represents the optimal balance of calculation speed and accuracy.
