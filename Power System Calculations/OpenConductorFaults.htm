Open Conductor Faults

Theoretical Review

Open conductor faults are series faults in a power system where one, two or even all three conductors of a power system element are disconnected, creating new unbalanced or balanced system state. It is a series fault unlike short circuits, which are shunt faults. The next figure illustrates all three cases:

  1. phase "a" is broken,
  2. phases "b" and "c" are broken, and
  3. all three phases are broken

As shown in the figure, the conductor(s) of the line between nodes "m" and "n" are broken at location "p".

CASE Implementation

Calculation of open conductor faults has been implemented in CASE as static calculation, like short circuits. CASE supports calculation of open conductor faults on lines, transformers, generators, and loads. By clicking "Open Conductors" button in the ribbon, the dialog window for calculation of open conductor faults is lunched. There are four tabs in the dialog, each for one type of power system element.
