Fourty Node Model

The simulation model includes transmission and distribution voltage levels from 400 kV down to 10 kV. There are four types of power plants in the model: hydro, thermal, solar, and wind. The wind power plant consists of three doubly-fed induction generating units. The model comprises the following power system elements:

  1. Fourty nodes - five 400 kV, fourteen 110 kV, one 35 kV, two 15.75 kV, four 10.5 kV, ten 10 kV, and four 0.69 kV.
  2. Seven synchronous generating units - three hydro units with salientâ€‘pole rotors and four thermal unit with a cylindrical (round) rotor;
  3. One static generator - used to model solar power plant;
  4. Four doubly-fed induction generators (DFIG) - used to model wind power plant;
  5. Eighteen transformers - two three winding and sixteen two winding;
  6. Twenty lines - five 400 kV, nine 110 kV, and six 10 kV;
  7. Fourteen loads [MW/Mvar] - 300/100, 240/80, 150/50/, 90/30, 60/20, 33/11, 30/10, 24/8, 21/7, 15/5, 15/5, 12/4, 1.5/0.5, 1.0/0.5. Total load consumption is: 992 MW and 331 Mvar
  8. External system which is system swing machine.

For transient stability calculation, three phase short circuit is simulated on the one of the lines between nodes Node 1 and Node 2. Fault occurs at t=1 s and is cleared at t=1.15 s by disconnecting the faulted line.
